Onboarding Guide — Sample Document

Welcome to the team. Your first week follows a structured onboarding plan
designed to get you productive quickly while making sure you understand how
the team works.

Day 1: IT setup and account provisioning. You will receive a laptop, email
account, and access to internal tools including the project tracker, chat
platform, and code repository. Complete the mandatory security awareness
training module before end of day.

Day 2-3: Product and codebase orientation. You will be paired with a buddy
from your team who will walk you through the codebase architecture, the
current sprint board, and the team's coding standards document. You are
expected to set up your local development environment and successfully run
the test suite by end of Day 3.

Day 4-5: First contribution. You will be assigned a small, well-scoped
starter task (typically a bug fix or minor feature) to complete and submit
for code review. This is meant to exercise the full development workflow:
branching, code review, CI checks, and deployment to the staging
environment.

Week 2 onward: Regular sprint participation. You will attend daily standups,
sprint planning, and retrospectives. Your manager will schedule a 30-day,
60-day, and 90-day check-in to discuss progress and any support you need.

Questions during onboarding should go to your assigned buddy first, and to
your manager or HR for anything role- or policy-related.
